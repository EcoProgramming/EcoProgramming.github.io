Eco is an upcoming modeling system from Princeton University, designed to make it easier to develop multi-paradigm lattice models and run many instances in parallel.

Eco is currently in a alpha state of development: while it is functional, it is far from complete, and there is not yet any documentation. Nevertheless, you may feel free to download it, and we will provide updates about development and projects. You are welcome to follow our progress on Pivotal Tracker (signup required).


What happened to eSLIME? The original name of this project, "eSLIME," reflected its origins as a modeling framework for microbial biofilms. We soon came to recognize that lattice-based complex systems models are used widely in other areas of ecology, physics and the social sciences. We chose the name "eco" to reflect this diversity of applications.
